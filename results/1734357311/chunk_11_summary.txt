The Indus Valley Civilization, thriving in semi-arid lands, relied on agriculture, though irrigation evidence is scarce outside Afghanistan.  While farming was key, animal husbandry was significant.  Horses, though present, weren't central to Harappan culture, unlike their extensive trade networks.  The economy's details remain partially obscure, hinting at a complex society supported by both agriculture and commerce.  Further research is needed to fully understand the intricacies of their economic systems.
