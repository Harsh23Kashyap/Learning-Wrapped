Mohenjo-daro, a significant Indus Valley Civilization site discovered in 1922, boasts remarkable structures like the Great Bath and Granary, along with artifacts including a bronze dancing girl and the Pasupati seal.  Further discoveries at Sutkagendor (1929) revealed a crucial trade link between Harappa and Babylon.  Chanhudaro (1931) showcased evidence of bead-making workshops, highlighting the city's craftsmanship.  These sites, along with Amri, offer glimpses into the sophisticated urban planning, artistry, and trade networks of the Indus Valley Civilization.
