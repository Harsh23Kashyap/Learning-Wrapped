Indus Valley Civilization's religion was rich and diverse, featuring a pantheon of gods and goddesses.  A key deity, identified as Pushpati Mahadeva, is depicted on seals in a yogic pose, surrounded by animals like elephants, tigers, and rhinoceroses, signifying power and dominion over nature.  The presence of numerous phallus and yoni symbols indicates a focus on fertility.  Tree and animal worship also played significant roles, showcasing a vibrant spiritual landscape connected to the natural world.
